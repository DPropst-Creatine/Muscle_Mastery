Lesson 2: Protein Requirements and Timing Strategies

Objective: Implement evidence-based protein intake protocols that support muscle protein synthesis and combat age-related anabolic resistance in middle-aged adults.

Key Learning Points

Adults over 40 require higher protein intake than the standard RDA, with research showing 1.1-1.6 g/kg body weight prevents muscle loss regardless of protein source5.

Higher protein intake provides a 35-50% lower risk of developing sarcopenia in women, with the protective effect being dose-dependent and source-independent5.

Strategic protein distribution throughout the day, including 25-30 grams per meal, optimizes muscle protein synthesis and supports resistance training adaptations6.

Plant-based protein sources can be equally effective as animal proteins when total intake reaches the higher recommended ranges for adults over 405.

Action Steps

  • 1. Calculate your protein needs: multiply your body weight in kg by 1.1-1.6
  • 2. Distribute protein across 3 main meals, aiming for 25-30g per meal
  • 3. Include a protein source at every meal and snack
  • 4. Track your protein intake for one week to establish baseline habits
