Lesson 4: Supplement Protocols and Meal Planning

Objective: Create personalized supplementation schedules and meal planning strategies that optimize muscle protein synthesis and energy metabolism for active adults over 40.

Key Learning Points

Creatine supplementation combined with resistance training produces synergistic effects on muscle mass, strength, and bone health in postmenopausal women over two-year periods9.

Timing creatine intake around workouts is less important than consistent daily consumption, with benefits accumulating through tissue saturation over 2-4 weeks10.

Meal planning should prioritize protein distribution across three main meals with 25-30 grams per meal to optimize muscle protein synthesis throughout the day6.

Combining adequate protein intake (>1.3 g/kg/day) with resistance training during weight loss phases preserves muscle mass and prevents metabolic slowdown11.

Action Steps

  • 1. Take 3-5g creatine daily at the same time (morning with breakfast works well)
  • 2. Plan 3 meals with 25-30g protein each, plus 1-2 protein-rich snacks
  • 3. Prep protein sources in advance: batch cook chicken, hard-boil eggs, portion greek yogurt
  • 4. Track your intake for 7 days to establish consistent patterns
