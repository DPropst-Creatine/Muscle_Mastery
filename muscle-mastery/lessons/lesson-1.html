Lesson 1: Creatine Science and Safety Fundamentals

Objective: Master the evidence-based foundation of creatine supplementation including safety profiles, optimal dosing, and quality selection for adults over 40.

Key Learning Points

Creatine monohydrate is supported by over 685 clinical trials with no significant safety concerns, making it one of the most researched and safest supplements available2.

Women naturally have 70-80% lower creatine stores than men and experience enhanced benefits from supplementation, especially during hormonal transitions after 402.

The standard 3-5 gram daily dose of creatine monohydrate provides optimal benefits without loading phases, with generic pharmaceutical-grade products being as effective as expensive branded alternatives3.

Creatine supplementation enhances cognitive function with significant improvements in memory (SMD = 0.31), attention time (SMD = -0.31), and processing speed (SMD = -0.51), with doubled benefits observed in adults over 654.

Action Steps

  • 1. Choose a pharmaceutical-grade creatine monohydrate supplement (look for USP or NSF certification)
  • 2. Start with 3-5 grams daily - no loading phase required
  • 3. Take consistently at the same time each day (timing relative to workouts is not critical)
  • 4. Track your energy levels and cognitive function over the first 2-4 weeks
