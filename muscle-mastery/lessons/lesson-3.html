Lesson 3: Power Training Over Strength Training

Objective: Design and implement power-focused resistance training programs that maximize functional capacity and reduce fall risk for middle-aged beginners.

Key Learning Points

Power training produces superior functional outcomes compared to traditional strength training, with research showing power is more predictive of mortality and independence than strength alone7.

Resistance training with 3+ sets at 70%+ of one-repetition maximum produces large effect sizes (SMD = 0.83-0.90) on strength improvements in adults with sarcopenia1.

Minimal effective dose protocols requiring just 10 minutes daily can produce significant muscle adaptations when performed consistently with progressive overload8.

Equipment-free resistance training using bodyweight exercises can effectively combat sarcopenia when structured with appropriate progression and intensity1.

Action Steps

  • 1. Focus on explosive movement patterns: jump squats, push-up claps, fast push-ups
  • 2. Perform 3 sets of 8-12 reps at 70-80% effort, emphasizing speed of movement
  • 3. Train 2-3 times per week with at least 48 hours between sessions
  • 4. Progress by increasing speed, adding resistance, or advancing exercise complexity
