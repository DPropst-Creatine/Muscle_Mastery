Teaser Lesson: Muscle Loss Reality Check

Objective: Understand the hidden costs of muscle loss and why action after 40 is critical for independence and vitality.

Sarcopenia begins as early as age 30 with 3-8% muscle loss per decade, accelerating after 40 and leading to falls, fractures, and loss of independence1.

Adults over 40 can reverse age-related muscle decline through evidence-based resistance training protocols that produce large effect sizes (SMD = 0.83-0.90) on strength improvements1.
